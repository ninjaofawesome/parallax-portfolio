How I Work.

The best work that I do sometimes never sees the light of my Github day. Yet each project that I am involved in is a valuable learning lesson and has shaped me into a better developer.

I love to write code, I love to learn, and I love to collaborate. I was part of the inaugural Full Stack Web Development Fellowship at the Flatiron School, and from day one I have only done the things that I love.

Each project that I work on gives me a chance to try new techniques, libraries and tools that I'm interested in. I enjoy a MEAN stack, asynchronous JavaScript, Angular, Ember, Sass, Bourbon, Neat and Bitters. Each project that I work on gives me a new opportunity to try a new approach, and revisiting previous ones oftentimes gives me the satisfaction of refactoring born of experience.

Although some of my most technically challenging work has been proprietary, I oftentimes find myself working on projects pro-bono that interest me, for people who are interesting to me. Check out some of my work below.
